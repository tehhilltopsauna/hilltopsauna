What is contrast therapy?

Heat. Cold. Rest. Repeat.

Contrast therapy is the practice of alternating between hot and cold environments — typically a sauna followed by a cold plunge, repeated over a session. The shift between extremes triggers a cascade of physiological responses that, over time, add up to real, measurable benefits.

At The Hilltop Sauna, that means moving between a wood-fired sauna at up to 90°C and open-air ice baths chilled to 5°C — with the hot tub and outdoor shower available to extend the ritual. The Cavehill hillside does the rest.

The benefits

What it does for you

Each benefit builds on the last. A single session leaves you feeling better. Regular sessions start to change things.

Circulation & cardiovascular health

Heat causes blood vessels to dilate, driving blood to the skin and muscles. Cold causes them to contract sharply, pushing blood back to the core. This repeated pumping action strengthens the vascular system over time — often compared to passive cardiovascular exercise.

Stress reduction & cortisol

Regular sauna use has been shown to lower baseline cortisol levels — the body's primary stress hormone. The cold plunge triggers a controlled stress response that, over time, trains the nervous system to handle stress more efficiently. Many regulars describe a lasting sense of calm after sessions.

Sleep quality

The drop in core body temperature that follows a sauna session closely mirrors what the body does naturally before sleep. Studies consistently link sauna use with improved sleep onset, deeper sleep stages, and higher overall sleep quality — particularly when sessions are taken in the late afternoon or evening.

Mood & mental clarity

Cold water immersion triggers a significant release of dopamine and norepinephrine — up to 300% above baseline in some studies. This produces a natural mood lift and heightened focus that can last for hours after a session. Many guests report leaving feeling clearer and more motivated than when they arrived.

Muscle recovery

The combination of heat — which relaxes muscles and increases blood flow — and cold — which reduces inflammation and soreness — makes contrast therapy a popular recovery tool among athletes. It helps flush metabolic waste from muscles and accelerates the repair process after physical exertion.

Immune function

Regular heat exposure has been linked to increased production of white blood cells and heat shock proteins — both markers of a more resilient immune system. Cold exposure adds to this by activating brown adipose tissue and triggering anti-inflammatory pathways that support long-term immune health.
